# Threats of Quantum Computing
Quantum computing poses a significant threat to widely used encryption systems. Here are some key points to consider:

* Quantum computing can break RSA, elliptic curve cryptography, and Diffie-Hellman key exchange encryption systems.
* Quantum algorithms like Shor's algorithm can render these problems trivial, dismantling the foundation of modern cryptographic security.
* Quantum computers can break RSA and ECC encryption within hours or even minutes, depending on the size and power of the quantum computer.
* AES encryption remains the most secure out of the three, but quantum computers can still crack it in a fraction of the time that classical computers can.

# Post-Quantum Cryptography
Post-quantum cryptography is designed to resist quantum attacks. Here are some key points to consider:

* The National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) has standardized post-quantum cryptographic algorithms.
* Quantum computing holds the potential for developing more secure quantum-resistant cryptography in the future.
* Post-quantum cryptography is a necessary step to ensure the security of data in the face of emerging quantum threats.

# Urgency and Timeline
The transition to quantum-resistant systems is urgent. Here are some key points to consider:

* The "harvest now, decrypt later" threat creates urgency for organizations handling sensitive long-term data.
* The "harvest now, decrypt later" strategy is already a concern, where attackers collect encrypted data today to decrypt in the quantum future.
* The time to transition to quantum-resistant systems is now.
* Within the next two decades, quantum computing is expected to present an unprecedented threat to modern encryption.
